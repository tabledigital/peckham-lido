Peckham Lido

Once a well-loved 50 metre outdoor swimming pool, Peckham Lido served swimmers and sun-bathers of South East London for over 60 years. By 1987 it had fallen into disrepair and was closed and filled in. All that remains visible is a crumbling blue fountain.

With many of Britain’s lidos coming back to life, the Peckham Lido Campaign (coordinated by Ben Lloyd-Ennals and James Glynn) has been set up to resurrect the South East London lido.

Now that the crowdfunding campaign has been a success (raising £65,000). The designs will be developed in collaboration with architects Studio Octopi, who are the founders of the Thames Baths Lido project.

The Lido will be rebuilt on the same site as the original, using the existing mature trees to frame the new buildings. A feasibility study is being conducted to look at delivering the following:

  • A 50 meter heated Olympic standard pool
  • Roof-top sunbathing areas
  • Sauna/Steam Room
  • Restaurant
  • Gym
  • Yoga/dance studio
  • Flexible community space

FAQs

Here are some questions we've been asked. Feel free to email us, tweet us, or join us on Facebook if you have a question we've not answered here!

Where is the Lido situated?

The lido is situated at the top of Peckham Rye Common between Peckham Rye and East Dulwich Road.

Are you working with Southwark Council and Mayor of London?

Yes, both Southwark Council and the Mayor of London have invested in the initial idea of the Lido and are committed to working with the Lido team to explore the feasibility of the Lido.

Will the Lido be a private business?

No, the Lido has been set up as a Community Interest Company, which means that it has directors, but profits are put back into the company.

How are you planning to preserve the existing trees?

We have instructed our architects to incorporate all the mature trees that currently stand on the site so that the Lido feels like it is part of the park, both inside and out.

Who will own the land the Lido sits on?

The land is currently designated as Metropolitan Open Land which is owned by Southwark Council. If planning is granted it likely that a trust would lease the land from the council; it would never be owned by the community interest company that ran the Lido.

When will the Lido likely be built?

We are hoping to have people splashing around in three years.

What about parking?

Parking is something that people on the surrounding streets care about deeply and we will have a careful plan about minimising the impact on local residents. As the Lido is situated in a very built up area we would encourage users to walk or cycle. We certainly don’t plan on putting a car park on the site, but we are planning lots of cycle parking.

Who are the Architects you are working with?

We are working with Studio Octopi, a practise based in South London who have a deep passion for outdoor swimming and lidos. Studio Octopi are also the instigators of the Thames Baths project.

What are the benefits for Peckham?

The Lido would bring an Olympic sized pool for local schools. It’s a healthy recreational facility for Peckham’s diverse community, serving all ages and cultures. It will bring health and wellbeing to the heart of the community and will create full and part time jobs that would suit a range of skill sets and qualifications, including life guards, personal trainers, yoga teachers, admin staff and more. Local shops, suppliers, restaurants and bars in Peckham, Nunhead and East Dulwich will benefit from the investment.

How can I help?

We would love to hear from you if you think you can help us or become part of the team. You can reach us on Twitter, Facebook or via email.

What are we up to?

The Peckham Lido campaign is working with Studio Octopi to produce drawings, updated visuals and technical documentation. Landscape architects are being commissioned to prepare landscape concept designs and quantity surveyors to prepare a construction cost estimate.

Public consultations are being held with the community in order to take on board ideas from locals who will then feed into the architect’s designs.

A business and commercial strategy is also being drafted as part of the feasibility study.

Peckham Lido is commissioning planning consultants to formally submit for Pre-Application Planning Advice to Southwark Council and is producing a business plan and commercial strategy for fundraising the construction costs and continuing business costs when the Lido is open.
